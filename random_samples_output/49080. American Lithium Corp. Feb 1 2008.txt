SUMMARY 

Soil sampling using the MMI technique was carried out in August 2006 along the strike 
extension of the known mineralization within the Reliance Property.  The purpose of the 
work was to determine potential of mineralization along the strike of the Royal shear zone, 
which is located within the northwestern part of the grid as well as to locate previously 
unknown mineralization. 

The Royal Zone was extensively drilled in 1987 by Menika and encountered significant gold 
mineralization along a strike length of about 650 meters.  In addition, the results of MMI soil 
sampling carried out in 2004 indicated the Royal Zone mineralization extends a further 450 
meters to the southeast for a total strike length of 1100 meters.  The grid for the August 2006 
MMI sampling is an extension of the 2004 and 2005 MMI sampling grids. 

MMI stands for mobile metal ions and describes ions, which have moved in the weathering 
zone and that are weakly or loosely attached to surface soil particles.  MMI, which requires 
special sampling and testing techniques, are particularly useful in responding to 
mineralization at depth, probably in excess of 700 meters.  It is characterized in having a 
high signal to noise ratio and therefore can provide accurate drill targets. 

The samples were taken along northeast-running lines with an interval of 100 meters and a 
sampling interval of 25 meters.  Each sample was taken at a depth from 10 cm to 25 cm, and 
placed in plastic zip-lock bags, which were then labeled with the station number and line 
number.  A total of 689 samples were taken along 16 lines. 

The samples were then sent to SGS Minerals in Toronto, Ontario for multi-element analysis 
consisting of 38 elements.  The lab results were sent to the writer who subsequently reduced 
the data as follows:  

1. The response ratios (essentially meaning the number times background) for eight 
metals was calculated and then stacked for each line in order to determine if any 
anomalies occur and what metals they were composed of. 

2. The MMI results for gold, copper, silver, arsenic, antimony, lead, zinc, and nickel 
were plotted on eight plan maps, respectively, and contoured. 

i 



CONCLUSIONS 

1. The MMI soil sampling located numerous very strong gold anomalies throughout the 
grid area with the strongest being 30,800 times background (1,540 ppb).  There are 
five main anomalous zones that have been labeled by the upper case letters A to E. 

2. Four of these anomalies, A, C, D, and E, have similar characteristics in that they 
consist of strong gold values which correlate with anomalous values in silver, copper, 
arsenic, and antimony, and they strike northwest-southeast.  The likely causative 
source is mesothermal vein-type mineralization, possibly similar to the Bralorne-
Pioneer mineralization. 

3. Of particular interest is the northwest end of anomaly A which consists of a widened 
zone of 150 meters along a strike length of 600 meters.  Anomaly A reflects the 
Imperial Zone, the diamond drilling of which suggests a resource of 80,000 ounces of 
gold along a strike length of 80 meters.  Therefore, this part of anomaly A suggests 
the Imperial Zone extends a further 520 meters than the known 80 meters.  The rest of 
anomaly A has a minimum strike length of up to 2600 meter being open to the 
northwest and to the southeast. 

4. Gold anomaly B, minimum strike length of 1300 meters and up to 200 meters wide, 
has different characteristics than the other gold anomalies since it correlates only with 
copper and silver but not with antimony and arsenic.  It also strikes easterly, rather 
than northwesterly.  The causative source is therefore probably different than that of 
other anomalies possibly a gold-mineralized intrusive. 

5. Nickel anomalies correlate with and along side of the gold anomalies.  The probable 
causative source is listwanite which is known to occur in the area and which often 
occurs close to gold mineralization such as at the Atlin Gold Camp and at the Mother 
Lode gold district in California. 

6. Anomaly F is a lead anomaly occurring within the northwestern part of the grid area.  
It is oval in shape having a size of 600 meters by 750 meters.  The only correlating 
metal is arsenic.

RECOMMENDATIONS 

 
1. The property should be mapped with an advanced GPS unit, that is, one that is 

accurate within a meter in both horizontally and vertically (elevation).  Particular 
attention should be paid to past diamond drill hole collars, trenches, roads, and the 
grid.  There are many GPS points on the grid but these were done with less accurate 
units and few measurements have been done on the drill holes and roads.  The 
purpose of carrying out this survey is to correlate the known mineralization with the 
MMI results as well as to assist in determining future drill hole locations. 

2. Fill in the MMI sampling within the southeastern part of the property, that is, on lines 
700W to 100E, by taking samples at 25-meter intervals on 100-meter lines.  The 
sampling in this area is reconnaissance in nature and thus the true shape of the 
causative source is unknown.   

3. Carry out diamond drilling on the MMI anomalies.  The anomalies considered to be 
of stronger exploration interest and therefore prime drill targets are anomaly A at its 
northwestern end and anomaly B within the southwestern part of line 800W.  These 
anomalies are definite targets at this point but the drill collar locations should not be 
determined until the GPS survey is completed.  Also the drilling could start before the 
MMI sampling is finished.

INTRODUCTION AND GENERAL REMARKS 

This report discusses survey procedure, compilation of data, interpretation methods, and the 
results of a mobile metal ion (MMI) survey carried out over the southern part of the Reliance 
Property belonging to Menika Mining Ltd.  The property is located on Steep Creek about 4 
km due east of the village of Gold Bridge within the Lillooet Mining Division, British 
Columbia. 

The general purpose of exploration on this property is to locate gold mineralization, similar 
to that of the nearby Bralorne Pioneer Mine, which is a mesothermal type consisting of gold 
with correlating sulphides within a vein consisting predominantly of quartz.  Historically, 
this mine produced 129.24 tonnes (4,115,066 troy ounces) of gold from 7.2 million tonnes 
grading 17.95 g Au/t.  Menika Mining, at lower elevations, had discovered several gold 
showings within three main mineralized trends.  One of these, the Royal Zone, contains the 
Imperial Zone which had been drilled to produce a resource of 2.46 million grams (79,000 
oz) gold (non 43-101 compliant due to the historic nature of the work).  Thus the more 
specific purpose is to extend the known mineralization as well as to discover previously 
unknown mineral zones.

lines 100E to 1000W, as well as detail MMI sampling carried out by Menika in 2003 on lines

LOCATION AND ACCESS 

The Reliance Property is located 3 km due east of the village of Gold Bridge on the 
northwestern slope of Mount Truax and on the southern shore of Carpenter Lake.   

The geographical coordinates for the center of the property are 50° 52' north latitude and 
122° 46' west longitude with the UTM coordinates, NAD 83, being 5635600 m N and 
615800 m E.  The NTS index is 92J/15, and the BCGS index is 92J087. 

Access is gained by traveling about 5 km northeasterly from Gold Bridge along the southern 
shore of Carpenter Lake on a narrow gravel road.  One then turns southerly to travel up the 
slope on a 4-wheel drive road to gain access to the various showings and drill sites.  Access 
to Gold Bridge is best via Lillooet, a small town 110 km to the east along a good gravel road.  
Lillooet is 323 km from Vancouver via the Fraser Canyon and Hope making the total 
distance to Gold Bridge from Vancouver 433 km.  A shorter route to Gold Bridge is via the 
Hurly River, but only in the summer time. 

PHYSIOGRAPHY 

The Reliance Property is found within the Pacific Ranges, which is a physiographic unit of 
the Coast Mountains.  The Pacific Ranges contain some of the highest peaks in the Coast 
Mountain system and the terrain is typically steep and rugged.  The peak of Mount Truax at 
2,880 meters (9,450 feet) is the highest in the area and is about 4 km southeast of the 
southeastern corner of the Reliance Property.  On the property itself, the elevations vary from 
654 meters (2145 feet) on Carpenter Lake which is on the northern part of the property to 
2,060 meters (6,760 feet) at the southeastern corner of the property to give a relief of 1,406 
meters (4,615 feet).  The terrain is steep over much of the property containing deeply incised 
stream valleys with the general slope facing northwesterly. 

The main water sources are Steep Creek, which flows northwesterly through the southern 
and western portions of the claims, and Carpenter Lake, which, as mentioned above, occurs 
along the northern boundary of the property.  A number of other unnamed northwesterly-
flowing creeks occur to the east of Steep Creek. 

Tree cover is formed mostly from lodge pole pine and Douglas fir.  Numerous downfall 
occur throughout the property making traversing difficult in places. 

The Reliance Property occurs on the boundary between West Coast Marine and Interior 
climatic zones being on the lee side of the Coast Mountains resulting in moderate 
precipitation.  The snowfall, however, is moderate to heavy usually exceeding three meters.  
The summers are warm and dry but can become, on occasion, quite hot.  The temperatures in 
the winters are usually below zero sometimes reaching in the minus 20's (centigrade).  
Exploration is curtailed in the winter due to the combination of snowfall and terrain.

d) Bralorne Mine 
The following was mostly taken from Dunn, et al's preliminary assessment report on 
the Bralorne Mine for Bralorne Mines Ltd, as reported on their web site. 

Historic production from the Bralorne and Pioneer mines on the property is recorded as 
129.24 tonnes (4,115,066 troy ounces) of gold from 7.2 million tonnes grading 17.95 g 
Au/t.  Silver production from the deposits is recorded as 29.61 tonnes (951,962 troy 
ounces), zinc as 297 kilograms (653 pounds) and lead as 216 kilograms (475 pounds).  
Minor scheelite production occurred during the Second World War.  Currently, the 
resources that are estimated to exist at the Bralorne Mine, consist of a measured 
resource of 14,000 tonnes grading 12 g Au/t and inferred resources of 125,300 tonnes 
grading 14.09 g Au/t. 

The deposits on the Bralorne Pioneer Gold Mine are members of a well recognized 
group of deposits referred to as mesothermal, orogenic or greenstone-hosted quartz-
carbonate gold vein deposits.  These deposits include the Mother Lode and Grass 
Valley districts in California and most of the greenstone hosted gold deposits in the 
Canadian Shield, including the Timmins-Val d'Or, Red Lake and Hemlo camps.  These 
deposits are quartz-carbonate veins in moderately to steeply dipping brittle-ductile 
shear zones and, locally, in shallow dipping extensional fractures.  

Veins on the Bralorne property are dominantly quartz, with minor carbonate minerals, 
mainly calcite and ankerite, and lesser amounts of chlorite, sericite, clay-altered 
mariposite, talc, scheelite and native gold.  Sulphides are present and, although locally 
abundant, make up less than 1% of total vein volume.  Pyrite and arsenopyrite are the 
most abundant sulphides with lesser marcassite, pyrrhotite, sphalerite, galena, 
chalcopyrite and rare tetrahedrite.  In the historic mining operations, approximately 
35% of the productive veins were ore grade above a 4.7 g Au/t cutoff. 

These veins are hosted in a Permian ophiolitic sequence, the Bralorne block of 
Cadwallader Terrane, and, northeast of the Ferguson Fault, in Triassic-Jurassic ribbon 
chert, argillite and basalt of the Bridge River Terrane.  The main control on vein 
emplacement is the bend in the regional Cadwallader Fault and the Ferguson Fault, a 
splay off of the Cadwallader Fault, which crosses the internal angle of the Cadwallader 
Fault.  These faults form a left lateral shear couple. En-echelon quartz veins formed in 
tension and shear fractures between and northeast of the bounding faults. 

Three types of shear veins are recognized on the company's property; fissure, tension 
and cross veins.  Fissure veins, including Veins 51, 55 and 77 at Bralorne, the Main 
Vein at Pioneer and the Peter Vein are the richest, widest and most continuous in the 
camp.  They have been traced continuously for up to 1500 metres along a 110° to 145° 

7 



strike and to a depth of 1800 metres down a steep northerly dip.  The fissure veins are 
commonly ribboned.  They have an average width of 1 to 1.5 metres but often pinch 
and swell, ranging from several centimeters to seven metres in width.  Tension veins 
are generally less continuous than the fissure veins with maximum strike lengths of 500 
metres and similar dip extensions.  They are characterized by massive white quartz 
with erratic high gold values, open-spaced filling textures, commonly including pockets 
of drusy to cockscomb quartz between widely spaced and slickensided septae.  They 
are usually not as rich as fissure veins and are hosted in fault sets that strike roughly 
70°and dip about 75° northwest.  These tension veins form oblique splays off of the 
fissure veins.  They include the 75 and 83 veins at Bralorne and the 27 vein at Pioneer. 
Cross veins are sub economic and are interpreted to be connecting structures between 
the fissure and tension veins. 

MMI SOIL SAMPLING 

a) Theory 
MMI stands for mobile metal ions and describes ions, which have moved in the 
weathering zone and that are weakly or loosely attached to surface soil particles.  MMI, 
which requires special sampling and testing techniques, are particularly useful in 
responding to mineralization at depth probably in excess of 700 meters.  The testing 
uses proprietary partial extraction techniques with specific combinations of ligands to 
keep metals in solution.  It also is not affected by glacial till, while standard soil sample 
techniques are.  MMI is characterized in having a high signal to noise ratio and 
therefore can provide accurate drill targets.  However, it may also move along fault 
lines and therefore could show the causative source to be laterally moved from where it 
actually is. 

b) Grid Emplacement and Sampling Procedure 
The 2006 soil sampling survey grid is an extension of the 2003 and 2005 soil sampling 
grid, mainly to the northwest. 

The base line for the grid was placed in a northwest direction at 700N, which is a 
change from the baseline being at 000N within the southeast part of the grid.  The 
survey lines then were emplaced every 100 meters perpendicular to the baseline in a 
northeast direction, while the sampling was being carried out, by blazing trees and by 
blaze orange flagging.  The sample spots, being every 25 meters, were marked by a 60-
cm wooden picket with an aluminum tag stapled to it and the grid coordinates marked 
thereon. 

The amount of MMI sampling during 2006 totaled 689 samples along 8,300 meters on 
a total of 16 lines.  The total is 1,013 samples for all three years, that is, 2004 to 2006. 

8 



The sampling procedure was to first remove the organic material from the sample site 
(A0 layer) and then dig a pit over 25 cm deep with a shovel.  Sample material was then 
scraped from the sides of the pit over the measured depth interval of 10 centimeters to 
25 centimeters.  About 250 grams of sample material was collected and then placed 
into a plastic Zip-loc sandwich bag with the sample location marked thereon.  The 689 
samples were then packaged and sent to SGS Minerals located at 1885 Leslie Street, 
Toronto, Ontario.  (This is only one of two labs in the world that do MMI analysis, the 
other being in Perth, Australia where the MMI method was developed.) 

c) Analytical Methods 
At SGS Minerals, the testing procedure begins with weighing 50 grams of the sample 
into a plastic vial fitted with a screw cap.  Next is added 50 ml of the MMI-M solution 
to the sample, which is then placed in trays and put into a shaker for 20 minutes.  (The 
MMI-M solution is a neutral mixture of reagents that are used to detach loosely bound 
ions of any of the 38 elements from the soil substrate and formulated to keep the ions in 
solution.)  These are allowed to sit overnight and subsequently centrifuged for 10 
minutes.  The solution is then diluted 20 times for a total dilution factor of 200 times 
and then transferred into plastic test tubes, which are then analyzed on ICP-MS 
instruments. 

Results from the instruments for the 38 elements are processed automatically, loaded 
into the LIMS (laboratory information management system which is computer software 
used by laboratories) where the quality control parameters are checked before final 
reporting. 

d) Compilation of Data 
Eight elements were chosen out of the 38 reported on and these were gold, silver, 
copper, lead, zinc, arsenic, antimony, and nickel.  The mean background value was 
calculated for each of the eight elements and this number was then divided into the 
reported value to obtain a figure called the response ratio.  Two stacked histograms 
(other than line 1100W which was divided into two halves because of its much greater

DISCUSSION OF RESULTS 
The MMI soil sampling has produced numerous gold anomalies, usually with very high 
values.  Five of the more prominent ones have been labeled by the upper case letters, A to E 
for ease of discussion.  A sixth anomaly is mostly a lead anomaly and has been labeled F. 

Anomaly A strikes northwest-southeast parallel to the baselines and approximately between 
stations 450N and 700N.  The anomaly extends from line 2500W to at least line 1100W and 
appears to continue to the southeast end of the grid at line 100E.  Therefore, it has a 
minimum strike length of 2600 meters being open to both the northwest and southeast.  The 
width varies from very narrow at 25 meters to 150 meters.  Anomaly A also has the highest 
values within the survey area with numerous values being above 100 ppb, which correlates 
with a response ratio of 2,000.  The highest value occurs at 2000W, 625N being 1,540 ppb, 
or a response ratio of 30,800. 

That part of the anomaly that has a width of 150 meters occurs on lines 1800W to 2400W, 
inclusive, thus having a strike length of 600 meters.  This part of the anomaly correlates with 
the Imperial Zone of which the previous drilling has indicated a resource of 80,000 ounces of 
gold (not 43-101 compliant) along a strike length of 80 meters.  Therefore, this suggests that 
the Imperial Zone extends a further 520 meters indicating much more mineralization than so 
far is known. 

This anomaly also consists of correlating anomalous values in copper, silver, arsenic and 
antimony indicating these metals occur with the gold mineralization. 

Anomaly B occurs within the southeastern part of the grid.  As opposed to the other four gold 
anomalies, this anomaly, or anomalous zone, strikes easterly, and correlates only with copper 
and silver, but not with arsenic and antimony.  It has a minimum strike length of 1300 meters 
being open to the east and west, and a width of up to 300 meters.  It has values up to 53.9 ppb 
which translates into a response ratio of 1078. 

The fact that this anomaly has different characteristics suggests that the causative source is 
different from that of the other anomalies.  Considering the probable correlation with known 

11 



mineralization, anomaly A is very likely caused by gold mineralization within a mesothermal 
vein.  Since anomalies C, D, and E have similar characteristics to A (strike direction of 
northwest and correlating anomalous values in gold, copper, silver, arsenic and zinc), these 
anomalies are also likely caused by the same source.  However, anomaly B probably has a 
different causative source, possibly an intrusive, because of, as mentioned above its different 
strike direction and its lack of correlation with antimony and arsenic. 

Anomaly C occurs southern corner of the property, strikes northwesterly, has a minimum 
strike length of 1,200 meters, and a width of up to 100 meters.  It is open to both the 
northwest and to the southeast.  It also is characterized by high values being up to 116 ppb 
which results in a response ratio of 2,320.  At its northwestern end, the anomaly crosses 
anomaly B and thus it is unknown in this area whether the causative source is that of B or C. 

Anomaly D occurs along the northeastern edge of the survey area to the immediate southwest 
of Camp Creek.  This anomaly is not as strong as the others reaching a high of only 32.1 
which translates into a response ratio of 642.  On most MMI surveys this would be highly 
significant.  Anomaly D strikes northwesterly, has a minimum strike length of 1300 meters 
and a width of up to 50 meters.  The width could be wider depending on how one defines the 
edges.  In addition, the width is open to the northeast in the area of lines 1700W to 2000W.  
Copper is particularly strong with good continuity within anomaly D. 

Anomaly E is a weaker anomaly striking northwest-southeast from line 2400W to at least 
line 800W between stations 800N and 900N.  There is evidence that it continues 
southeasterly to line 100W but in this area, anomaly B also occurs which cross strikes 
anomaly E.  On some lines anomaly E can barely be seen but on other lines it has strong 
values that are up to 14.2 ppb (284 response ratio) on line 1800W at 875N.  This anomaly 
may be reflecting the Treasure Zone at its northwest end. 

Anomaly F is a lead anomalous zone that is centered at 1900W at 1150N.  The only metal 
that significantly correlates with this anomaly is arsenic.  It is oval-shaped with dimensions 
of about 600 meters northwest by about 750 meters northeast.  It would appear that this 
anomaly reflects lead mineralization but there is no correlating of typical metals that 
correlate with lead such as silver and zinc. 

Nickel anomalies occur within the southeastern part of the grid as well as along the 
southwestern and northeastern edges of the grid.  These anomalies often correlate with or 
along side of the gold anomalies, but not on every line.  It is probable that the nickel is 
reflecting basic and/or ultra-basic rock-types.  In this case, it is probably listwanite which 
Gordon Richards, geologist, (personal communication) says occurs throughout the 
mineralized area.  It should be noted that this is a common rock-type that occurs in most 
world class gold camps such as at Atlin and the mother lode area of California.
